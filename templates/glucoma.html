Glaucoma Disease

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often due to increased pressure within the eye. If left untreated, glaucoma can cause vision loss and blindness.

Symptoms

  • Patchy blind spots in peripheral or central vision
  • Tunnel vision in advanced stages
  • Severe eye pain
  • Headaches
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Blurred vision

Treatment

Treatment for glaucoma aims to reduce intraocular pressure to prevent further damage to the optic nerve. This can include:

  • Medications (eye drops, pills) to lower intraocular pressure
  • Laser therapy to improve fluid drainage from the eye
  • Surgery to create a new drainage channel for the eye fluid

Regular eye examinations are crucial for early detection and treatment of glaucoma, as the condition often presents without noticeable symptoms until irreversible vision loss has occurred.
