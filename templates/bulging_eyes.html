Bulging Eyes Disease

What is Bulging Eyes Disease?

Bulging eyes, also known as proptosis or exophthalmos, is a condition where one or both eyes protrude from the eye sockets more than normal. It can be a sign of various underlying health conditions.

Symptoms

  • Eyes that appear to bulge or protrude from the eye sockets
  • Swelling or redness around the eyes
  • Difficulty closing the eyes completely
  • Eye pain or discomfort
  • Blurred or double vision
  • Dryness or irritation of the eyes

Treatment

Treatment for bulging eyes depends on the underlying cause and severity of the condition. It may include:

  • Management of underlying health conditions such as thyroid disorders, infections, or tumors.
  • Medications to reduce inflammation or control thyroid function.
  • Surgery to correct the position of the eyes, relieve pressure on the optic nerve, or remove any underlying tumors or masses.
  • Eye lubricants or artificial tears to relieve dryness and discomfort.

Early diagnosis and treatment of bulging eyes are important to prevent complications such as vision loss or damage to the optic nerve.
