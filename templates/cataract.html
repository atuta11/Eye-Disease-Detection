Cataract Disease

What is Cataract Disease?

Cataract is a common eye condition characterized by the clouding of the eye's natural lens. This cloudiness causes blurred vision and can interfere with daily activities.

Symptoms

  • Blurry or dim vision
  • Difficulty seeing at night
  • Sensitivity to light and glare
  • Seeing halos around lights
  • Double vision in one eye
  • Frequent changes in glasses or contact lens prescriptions

Treatment

The primary treatment for cataracts is surgical removal of the cloudy lens. This surgery involves replacing the clouded lens with an artificial intraocular lens (IOL), restoring clear vision. Cataract surgery is safe and highly effective, with millions of procedures performed worldwide each year.

In cases where surgery is not immediately necessary or feasible, lifestyle changes such as wearing sunglasses with UV protection, quitting smoking, managing diabetes, and maintaining a healthy diet rich in antioxidants may help slow the progression of cataracts.
