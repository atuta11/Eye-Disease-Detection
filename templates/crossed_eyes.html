Crossed Eye Disease

What is Crossed Eye Disease?

Crossed eyes, medically known as strabismus, is a condition where both eyes do not look at the same place at the same time. While one eye looks forward to focus on an object, the other eye may turn inward, outward, upward, or downward.

Symptoms

  • Eyes that appear misaligned
  • Double vision
  • Squinting or closing one eye in bright sunlight
  • Difficulty with depth perception
  • Eyestrain or headaches, particularly when reading or doing close work

Treatment

Treatment for crossed eyes depends on the underlying cause and severity of the condition. It may include:

  • Eye exercises and vision therapy to improve eye coordination and strengthen eye muscles.
  • Prescription glasses or contact lenses to correct refractive errors and improve vision.
  • Eye patches or occlusion therapy to encourage the use of the weaker eye.
  • Surgery to adjust the muscles around the eye and realign the eyes, particularly if other treatments are ineffective or if there is a structural abnormality causing the crossed eyes.

Early intervention and treatment of crossed eyes are important to prevent complications and maintain good vision and eye health.
