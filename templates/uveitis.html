Uveitis Disease

What is Uveitis Disease?

Uveitis is inflammation of the uvea, the middle layer of the eye. It can affect the iris, ciliary body, and choroid, causing various symptoms and complications.

Symptoms

  • Eye redness
  • Eye pain or discomfort
  • Blurred or decreased vision
  • Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
  • Floaters or spots in vision
  • Eye floaters or spots
  • Eyes that appear cloudy or hazy
  • Eye discharge

Treatment

Treatment for uveitis aims to reduce inflammation, relieve symptoms, and prevent complications. It may include:

  • Topical or oral corticosteroids to reduce inflammation and pain.
  • Immunosuppressive medications to suppress the immune response and prevent recurrence.
  • Eye drops or ointments to dilate the pupil and relieve pain.
  • Anti-inflammatory medications such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) or biologic agents.
  • Surgery in severe cases to remove scar tissue or repair complications such as glaucoma or cataracts.

Early diagnosis and prompt treatment of uveitis are essential to prevent vision loss and preserve eye health.
